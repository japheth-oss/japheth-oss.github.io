Catholic Schools of Thought Test

Note: This test explores various Catholic philosophical and theological traditions.

What is this test?

This test attempts to identify your alignment with various Catholic schools of thought such as Thomism, Scotism, Augustinianism, Molinism, and others. You will be presented with statements, and you will answer with your opinion from Strongly Agree to Strongly Disagree, with each answer affecting your scores. At the end of the quiz, your answers will be compared to the maximum possible for each school, giving you a percentage. Answer honestly!

What are the schools of thought?

Thomism - Based on St. Thomas Aquinas, emphasizes the real distinction between essence and existence, the priority of intellect over will, the analogy of being, and intrinsic efficacy of grace.
Scotism - Based on Blessed John Duns Scotus, emphasizes univocity of being, the priority of will over intellect, the formal distinction, and the theological necessity of the Immaculate Conception.
Augustinianism - Based on St. Augustine, emphasizes the corruption of the will by original sin, the necessity of irresistible grace, predestination not based on foreseen merits, and divine illumination.
Molinism - Based on Luis de Molina, emphasizes middle knowledge (scientia media), libertarian free will, and predestination based on foreknowledge rather than prior decrees.
Banezianism - Dominican school emphasizing physical premotion, predestination prior to foreknowledge, and grace efficacious by God's action rather than human cooperation.
Bonaventurianism - Based on St. Bonaventure, emphasizes divine illumination, Platonic metaphysics, the inseparability of intellectual and affective knowledge, and the Franciscan spiritual tradition.

Nominalism - Based on William of Ockham, emphasizes that universals are merely mental constructs, God's will as the foundation of morality, and the separation of faith and reason.
Lullism - Based on Ramon Llull, emphasizes a universal logical-combinatorial method for evangelization, demonstration of theological truths through rational diagrams, and systematic combination of divine attributes.
Latin Averroism - Emphasizes the autonomy of philosophy from theology, the possibility of parallel truths, and Aristotelian philosophy as the best description of the natural world, even if contrary to theology.
